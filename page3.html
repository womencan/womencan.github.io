ARIANA GRANDE GOT SLAMMED FOR WEARING MINIDRESS ON ARETHA FRANKLIN FUNERAL AND GROPED BY PASTOR CHARLES H. ELLIS III

by Alaiza Raine M. San Pedro

Ariana Grande stirred up controversy after wearing a minidress to Aretha Franklin's funeral. The popstar performed at Greater Grace Temple on August 31, 2018 in Detroit, Michigan doing her rendition of Franklin's "Natural Woman."

While many praised her for her vocal ability, people came to twitter to bash her for her outfit, wearing a sleeveless black minidress with a mesh panel at her chest.

Following her time on stage, in which she was groped by Pastor Charles H. Ellis III, the internet went crazy about what they deemed to be a very inappropriate church attire. 

"Ariana Grande's ponytail is longer than her dress," wrote one critic. One critic even went far to suggest that the hemline of her dress was merely classless. Some critics even said that she deserved being groped by the said pastor (who later apologized) because of what she wore and it seems like she was asking for it.

A lot of fans went to social media to defend the Grammy award-winning singer.  One fan said, "Friendly reminder: a short dress is not an invitation."

In this world where toxic masculinity still exists everyone must stop victim blaming. No matter what women wear, no matter where women are, WOMEN SHOULD BE RESPECTED.
