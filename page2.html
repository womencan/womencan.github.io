6 Celebrities Who Have Fought Back Against Body Shaming 

It is so easy for people to shame someone for her body especially today, in the digital world. Scroll down to see what your favorite celebrities have to say about this. 

by Kit Collete V. Acuna

1. Bela Padilla

After posting a bikini picture of her in her vacation in Amanpulo, the Meet Me in St. Gallen star received a lot of bashful comments on her instagram post. In response, she tweeted, "Would it have made you more comfortable if I edited my photo to perfection? Because I could have easily done that. But what message does it send to the younger ones? That our photos are only likeable when we're perfect in photos? Not from me. NEVER."

2. Lili Reinhart

The riverdale star was said to be pregnant after photos of her and boyfriend Cole Sprouse circulated in the internet. In response, she shut down her body shamers by writing on instagram stories, "Nope. Not Pregnant." She added, "My body is something that I will NEVER apologize for. My body will constantly go through change. And so will yours. And that's fine. So let's not put so much time and effort into caring about a stranger's figure."

YES Lili! We couldn't agree more!

3. Kim Kardashian

After giving birth, she received hates from body shamers. Shutting down her body shamers, she tweeted, "...Making fun of me pregnant and making fun of me trying to lose weight now, shame on you. I'm not perfect but I will never conform to your skinny standards sorry!"

Selena Gomez

When one instagram user tried to hate on Selena's scars after kidney transplant, she shut down her body shamers and posted a response on her instagram.

"The beauty myth: an obsession with physical perfection that traps the modern woman in an endless cycle of hopelessness, self consciousness and self-hatred as she tries to fulfill society's impossible definition of flawless beauty," she wrote in her caption. "I chose to take care of myself because I want to, not to prove anything to anyone. Wind in her sails."

Ashley Graham

On Instagram, she recalled a time that someone once called her thighs "cellulite city", to which she shot back: "But I now realize these thighs tell a story of victory and courage." Graham continues to push for the normalization of all body types.

Emma Stone 

Emma Stone has had trolls go after her for looking too skinny. In an interview with USA Today, she said, "I firmly believe that nothing really affects you or can really bother you if you don't already feel that way about yourself."
