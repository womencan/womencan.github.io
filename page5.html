A ONE-ON-ONE INTERVIEW WITH A FEMINIST

Read this interview regarding feminism and the injustices experienced by a woman herself.

On the 9th of September, Shaira Joy M. Paclarin, a Passenger Service's Agent at United Airlines, was interviewed by our team regarding the topic about Women Empowerment. On her 22 years of existence, she has seen the injustices towards women. When asked about her experiences towards these injustices she said, "I am a victim of body shaming myself. Lagi nilang sinasabi na huy shaira ang payat mo na kumain ka nga para kang buto. Sometimes diba when you see photos sa instagram parang naiinsecure ka. But then, this body is a gift from God. Iba iba tayo. I know, we are all beautiful eh in our own ways. You might be bigger than what the society tells you so ano? Are you going to abide by these standards? I am skinny and I am beautiful. Your body is something that you should be proud of."

The team couldn't agree more. Our body is truly our temples. Kit, one of the the developers, asked Ms. Shaira, besides body shaming if she encountered things such as slut shaming and victim blaming . "Ay oo especially slut shaming. Our clothing preference, hindi naman iyan reason or invitation para bastusin ka. It's my body, it should be my choice diba? Kahit nga sobrang tago na yung skin natin nasisipulan pa rin tayo diba? Most especially mga truck driver na may pa-hi miss pa, sometimes di tayo makapagpigil I actually raise my middle finger at them. Konting control naman hoy! Tapos pag na-share mo especially sa matatanda sasabihin nila na baka maikli suot ko. It's 2018 na, why is this still a thing?"

She even tackled the issues about acne shaming. "Since college ako talaga marami nakong pimples. I tried a lot of products pero wala talaga dumarami lang sila. You know what? It ruined my self-esteem.

Kasi makikita ko yung barkada ko parang bakit ang kikinis nila? This thing should be normalized talaga. Diba normal naman yun eh? Kaso yung makikita mo kasi sa media puro unrealistic diba nakakita ka ba ng model ng damit na may acne? Wala so it really ruined my confidence. Para bang ang panget panget mo."

Alaiza, one of our developers, asked Ms. Shaira what does she think is the way to stop this toxic masculinity. She responded with, "Ako sa tingin ko, it should start with women ourselves. Kasi diba parang minsan kapwa babae pa yung nagda-down sa iba pang babae. Why don't we respect each other instead?"

Sean, one of the developers, asked what is the message that she wants to give to the youth regarding feminism. "Feminism kasi it's not about women being superior. Kasi it's about equality. And for women, huwag natin hayaan na i-control ng society yung way of living natin. Gusto mo mag-minidress? Go. Gusto mo magshorts? Go. Gusto mo mag-sobrang habang damit? Go. It is your choice. Don't dress for anybody but yourself. And learn the basic concept of respect. Yan naman yon eh respeto talaga. Respect must be given to all men and women, diba? It must be equal. Equality. That is feminism."

One of the highlights of this interview was when Ms. Shaira said, "Women can do everything. Pareho lang tayong lahat na tao. Your gender nor sexuality isn't something that you should be sorry for. Be proud of who you are. You might be God's greatest gift to anyone hindi mo lang alam."
