WOMEN WHO WERE SLUT-SHAMED FOR SIMPLY EXISTING

by Sean Ashley Junio

That time Ariel Winters wore shorts in public. Last summer, Ariel Winter stepped out in a pair of denim short-shorts — and immediately, she was slut-shamed for *gasp* wearing shorts in the summer heat.

However, Winter didn't take the criticism lightly. She quickly defended her outfit choice in a scathing Twitter rant.

That time Sarah Hyland cuddled with her boyfriend. Sarah Hyland was attacked after posting an adorable selfie with her boyfriend Wells Adams (and their dog) because they were laying in a bed. 

That time Bella Thorne wore a cropped sweatshirt. In September, blogger Perez Hilton slut-shamed Bella Thorne for wearing a cropped hoodie out in public.

Hilton reposted a paparazzi snapshot of Thorne onto his own Instagram and tagged her exes. He captioned the photo, "Fill in the blank! Bella Thorne __________," prompting his followers to slut-shame Thorne (all because she wore a cut-off sweatshirt). 

After Nicki’s split from Meek Mill earlier this year, the slut shaming comments went into full force. Meek claimed that he never trusted her while they were together and even mocked her plastic surgery while Rick Ross said he told Meek not to get with a girl like he — which leads people to speculate about what kind of chick Minaj really is. As for Nicki, she’s sitting pretty, unbothered and plotting her next money bag.

Madge was getting shamed for her outfits and sexy routines way before some of the ladies on this list were even born. She was the bad girl of pop and continues to push boundaries, even today as a seasoned entertainer. Her VMA kiss with Britney Spears and Christina Aguilera added a more energy to her invisible scarlet letter and made people slut shame her even more. Thankfully, Madonna listened to her own inner voice and remained her risque self, which ultimately made her the icon that she is today.

It’s sad how it's already 2018 yet double standards still exists. Like, if a woman is spotted out with more than one guy in a week — whether it’s their first time meeting or they’re working together — she’s considered promiscuous. However, if a guy does it, he’s considered “the man.”
But it’s a new day and women want equality, no matter how you slice it. These celebrity women refuse to be chastised for doing the same things that their male counterparts.
